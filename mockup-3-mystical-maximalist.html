Evangeline Adams kept rooms at Carnegie Hall and cast charts for Morgan, Mary Pickford, King Edward VII, and the Duponts. In 1914 she was tried in New York for fortune-telling and acquitted — after reading the judge's own chart from the witness stand. Her notes on the 1907 Panic were dated months before it occurred.
